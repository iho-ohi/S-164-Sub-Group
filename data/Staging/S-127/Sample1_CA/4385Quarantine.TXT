Quarantine Messages 
1.  In the following circumstances,  and per Canadian  legal requirements,  the person in charge of a vessel shall inform a quarantine officer prior to arrival where, in the course of the voyage:  
A. A person on board has the following signs and symptoms 
a)  Fever of 38°C or greater OR signs of fever (e.g., shivering, flushed skin, excessive sweating, complains of feeling feverish); 
b)  Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath; 
c)  New or worsening cough 
d)  Cough with blood 
e)  Headache; 
f)   Recent confusion; 
g)  Skin rash; 
h)  Bruising or bleeding without previous injury;  
i)   Fatigue or weakness;  
j)   Muscle or body aches;  
k)  New loss of smell or taste;  
l)   Gastrointestinal symptoms (abdominal pain, diarrhea, vomiting); 
m) Obviously unwell.  

B. There is a death on board. 
C. Human remains are on board 

2.  To  inform  a  quarantine  officer,  the  person  in charge  of a vessel shall  contact  the  Public  Health Agency of Canada’s Central Notification System (CNS) by email at phac.cns-snc.aspc@canada.ca. Notification  is  required  as  soon  as  possible  prior  to  arrival.  Should  there  be  any  change  in  the situation being reported, an updated report must be sent. 
3.  All notifications (new or updated) submitted 24 hours or less prior to arrival must be accompanied 
by a call to CNS at 1-833-615-2384. 
4.  The person in charge of a vessel who wishes to change  their port of destination after receiving 
instructions from a quarantine officer shall notify the quarantine officer of such a change and request 
new instructions.
